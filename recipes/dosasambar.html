Dosa and Sambar



Dosa is a thin, crispy fermented crepe made from rice and lentils. Sambar is a flavorful lentil-based vegetable stew made with tamarind and aromatic spices. Together, they form a nutritious, comforting, and protein-rich meal that’s loved across India.

Ingredients

    For the dosa batter :

  • 2 cups rice (regular or parboiled rice)
  • ½ cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 tbsp chana dal (optional – helps in crispiness)
  • ¼ tsp fenugreek seeds (methi – aids fermentation)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking dosa)

    • For Sambar :

    Main Ingredients :
  • ¾ cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 small onion (or shallots)
  • 1 medium tomato
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, drumstick, pumpkin, etc.)
  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind (soaked in warm water)
  • 2–3 cups water (adjust for desired consistency)
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ tsp turmeric
    • For Sambar Masala :
  • 2 tsp sambar powder (store-bought or homemade)
    • For Tempering (Tadka) :
  • 1–2 tsp oil or ghee
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 dry red chili
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Recipe

    Dosa Recipe

  1. Rinse and soak rice, urad dal, chana dal, and methi seeds for 4–6 hours.
  2. Drain and grind to a smooth batter using water as needed. Batter should be pourable.
  3. Let the batter ferment in a warm place overnight (8–12 hours). It should rise and turn slightly bubbly.
    4. Make Dosa :
  4. Mix the batter, add salt, and heat a tawa.
  5. Pour a ladle of batter, spread it in a circular motion.
  6. Drizzle oil/ghee around edges.
  7. Cook until golden and crispy. No need to flip.
  8. Serve hot!

    9. Sambar Recipe

    Cook Dal :
  9. Rinse toor dal, add turmeric, and pressure cook until soft and mushy (3–4 whistles).
  10. Mash well and set aside.
    11. Prepare Tamarind Water :
  11. Soak tamarind in warm water, extract juice, and discard pulp.
    12. Cook Veggies :
  12. In a pan, boil chopped vegetables with a little salt until soft.
  13. Add chopped tomatoes and simmer.
    14. Combine :
  14. Add the mashed dal to the cooked veggies.
  15. Pour in tamarind extract and sambar powder.
  16. Let it simmer for 5–10 minutes.
    17. Tempering (Tadka) :
  17. Heat oil/ghee, add mustard seeds, dry chili, curry leaves, and hing.
  18. Pour this tempering over the simmering sambar.
    19. Garnish :
  19. Add fresh coriander leaves to sambar.
