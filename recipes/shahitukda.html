Shahi Tukda



Shahi Tukda is a rich and indulgent Mughlai dessert made with deep-fried bread slices soaked in sugar syrup and topped with thickened saffron-flavored milk (rabri), nuts, and sometimes edible silver leaf. The name literally means "royal piece" – and it truly lives up to it with its decadent flavor and texture. It’s often served at festivals or special occasions.

Ingredients

    For the bread base :
  • 6 slices of white bread (preferably slightly stale)
  • Ghee or oil (for deep frying)
    • For rabri (thickened milk topping) :
  • 1 liter full cream milk
  • ¼ cup condensed milk (optional, for extra richness)
  • 2-3 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • A few saffron strands (soaked in warm milk)
  • ½ tsp cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios, cashews)

Recipe

    step 1 : Make the rabri
  1. Boil the milk in a wide pan. Lower the heat and simmer, stirring frequently.
  2. Keep scraping the sides and add the malai (cream) back to the milk.
  3. Once reduced to about ⅓, add sugar, saffron milk, cardamom powder, and condensed milk (if using).
  4. Cook for another 5-10 minutes. Set aside to cool slightly.
    5. step 2 : Prepare the sugar syrup
  5. In another pan, combine sugar, water, cardamom, and saffron.
  6. Boil until it slightly thickens (1-string consistency is not necessary).
  7. Add rose or kewra water. Turn off the heat.
    8. step 3 : Fry the bread
  8. Cut bread slices into triangles or squares.
  9. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Deep fry the bread until golden and crisp.
  10. Place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
    11. step 4 : Assemble the Shahi Tukda
  11. Dip fried bread pieces in warm sugar syrup for 30 seconds.
  12. Arrange them on a serving plate.
  13. Pour rabri generously over the syrup-soaked bread.
  14. Garnish with chopped nuts and more saffron (or silver leaf if available).
